Prefixes and Suffixes


Prefixes are added to the beginning of words and change their meanings.

1. Pre-: Before.
Example: "Preheat" means to heat something before you cook it.

2. Re-: Again.
Example: "Reread" means to read something again.

3. Mis-: Wrong or badly.
Example: "Misunderstand" means to understand something wrongly.

Suffixes are added to the end of words and can change their meanings or tell us more about them.

1. -ful: Full of.
Example: "Beautiful" means full of beauty.

2. -less: Without.
Example: "Fearless" means without fear; very brave.

3. -ly: In a certain way.
Example: "Happily" means doing something in a happy way.

4. -ment: The act of doing something or the result of an action.
Example: "Achievement" means the result of achieving something; success.
